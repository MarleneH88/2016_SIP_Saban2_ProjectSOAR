Why Awareness and Prevention is Important:

<<<<<<< HEAD

When talking about sexual assault, awareness is a crucial aspect of prevention. Prevention is a long road that has no shortcuts. In order for prevention to be effective, it must have a multifaceted approach.

Awareness

College students have the right to feel safe on their campus.

<<<<<<< HEAD

They also have the right to know there are reasons not to.

A primary obstacle we are facing in combating college campus sexual assault is the lack of awareness in the general population. If we don't know the problem exists, we can't solve it. Awareness is also inhibited by the stigma developed through preconceived notions about college campus sexual assault.

There are many presumptions about the causes of college campus sexual assault. Victim blaming remains a huge reason why victims of sexual assault don't report. Alcohol, clothing choices, or walking alone at night shouldn't be justifications or reasonings for sexual assault to occur.

Having colleges publish truthful statistics about the dangers of sexual assault on campus is imperative in the fight against college campus sexual assault. Students have the right to know about sexual assault incidents that have happened on their campus, and if there are repeat offenders walking amongst them.

Reporting these Crimes

About 80% of college campus rapes go unreported. Less than 5% are reported to law enforcement.

<<<<<<< HEAD

But why does this happen?

Commonly cited reasons for not reporting are reprisal, belief that the police would or could not do anything, and fear that the crime is not serious enough.

Too often our society suggests that sexual assaults are not serious, the fault of the victim, or unevidenced and thus unable to be punishable. In order for the statistics on reported assaults to change, we need to support survivors. We need to believe their stories, and demand consequences for their assailants. Project SOAR looks to help survivors by providing new ways of support through online story-sharing, a safer reporting method, and a community that says "We believe you. We support you. We value you."

False Statistics

Colleges continue to think it is acceptable to publish false statistics.

<<<<<<< HEAD

91% of colleges reported 0 cases of sexual assault in 2014.

Colleges can take a huge step in the right direction by owning up to what is happening on campuses all across the country. The part we play as citizens is demanding truthful numbers on college campus sexual assault.

In many sexual assault cases, colleges would rather hand out suspensions, probations, or warnings than expulsions, even though these crimes would be punishable under the law.

Colleges have the power to sweep sexual assaults under a rug, protecting the assailants. Out of the small number of students who reported their assaults, many have said that the way the school handled it made them feel worse than experiencing the actual assault.

Colleges can make a profound difference in the fight against sexual assault by simply providing truthful statistics, taking cases of sexual assault seriously, and proactively looking to make their campuses a better, safer place for students.

Through Project SOAR's story-sharing feature, we look to not only build a community of survivors, but also to show the multitude of students who have experience sexual assault. The stories quantifiably show that the numbers colleges make public are in no way representative of what is happening on campuses every day.
